🍃 Leaf 002 — Water Doesn’t Fight the Stone

Ages 5–8 · Themes: patience, endurance, gentleness

Kid Story (read aloud)

A little stream met a big stone. The stone was hard. The stream was soft.

The stream did not shout, “Move!” It did not fight. It just kept flowing—one day at a time.

Days became months. Months became years. The stone stayed strong… but the water stayed steady.

The pond whispered: “Time can wear down stone. Gentle things can be powerful— when they don’t stop.”

What it teaches

Gentle endurance is real strength.

You don’t need to fight to make progress. You need to keep going.

Word Pond (3 words)

gentle = soft, not mean endurance = keep going for a long time steady = same good pace every day

Ripple Question

What is something you can practice a little bit every day?

Play Together (2–5 minutes)

Drip Drip Wins (no mess version)

  1. Put a book on the table: that’s the “stone.”
  2. Tap the table next to it slowly with one finger: “drip… drip… drip…”
  3. After 20 taps, say together: “Gentle and steady.”
  4. Ask: “Did we need to fight the stone?”

Optional: if you want, use a spoon and a cup of water over the sink—one drip at a time.
